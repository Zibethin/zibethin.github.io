About Me

Users and impact sit at the core of the work that I do, be it to support a decision-maker, a board meeting, a newsletter recipient, or a person affected by a disaster.
Pragmatism and agility drive my ways of working, be it in a crowded office or remotely in a different timezone.
I am a creative problem solver and approach challenges with a double diamond mindset whether it is about collecting needs on the ground after a disaster, crunching data to ensure decision-makers receive timely information, or designing a model to change our ways of working.
After completing my PhD in Nanotechnology from the London Centre for Nanotechnology, I deliberately shifted to work on the front-line and used my planning and analysis skills to manage information in highly volatile environments. You can find my work on Ebola in the Democratic Republic of the Congo featured various newspapers and blogs linked below.

Selected Products and Projects

Information management for coordination of shelter activities
In 2019, Hurricane Dorian hit the islands of Abaco and Grand Bahama near the coast of Florida, causing most structures on the islands to be flattened or swept at sea, leaving nearly 100,000 people without a home. I worked with the Shelter cluster team to provide information to coordinate the efforts of NGOs on the ground in the form of analyses of capacity, geographic targeting, and damage assessments.
